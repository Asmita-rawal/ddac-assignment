Essential Safety Tips

Practical strategies to keep your loved ones safe and prevent wandering.

🏠 Home Safety

  • 1
    Secure Doors and Windows

    Install locks that are out of sight or require a key. Use door alarms or sensors to alert you when a door is opened.

  • 2
    Remove Tripping Hazards

    Clear pathways of clutter, rugs, and cords. Ensure good lighting in all areas, especially hallways and stairs.

  • 3
    Safe Outdoor Spaces

    Create a secure outdoor area with fencing. Remove dangerous items like sharp tools or poisonous plants.

  • 4
    Kitchen Safety

    Install stove guards and automatic shut-off devices. Keep knives and dangerous appliances out of reach.

📱 Technology & Tools

  • 1
    GPS Tracking Devices

    Use wearable GPS trackers that allow you to locate your loved one in real-time if they wander.

  • 2
    Medical Alert Systems

    Consider emergency response buttons that can be worn as pendants or bracelets for quick assistance.

  • 3
    ID Bracelets

    Ensure your loved one wears an ID bracelet with their name, condition, and emergency contact information.

  • 4
    Video Monitoring

    Install cameras in common areas to keep an eye on your loved one when you're not in the room.

👥 Community & Care

  • 1
    Build a Support Network

    Inform neighbors, friends, and family about your loved one's condition and how they can help.

  • 2
    Join Support Groups

    Connect with other caregivers to share experiences, tips, and emotional support.

  • 3
    Regular Check-ins

    Schedule regular visits or calls from family and friends to monitor well-being and provide social interaction.

  • 4
    Caregiver Respite

    Take regular breaks to avoid burnout. Consider respite care services for temporary relief.

📋 Emergency Preparedness

  • 1
    Create a Response Plan

    Have a clear plan for what to do if your loved one wanders. Include a list of places to check and people to contact.

  • 2
    Keep Recent Photos

    Maintain up-to-date photos of your loved one to share with authorities and community members.

  • 3
    Medical Information

    Keep a file with medical history, medications, and doctors' contact information readily accessible.

  • 4
    Notify Authorities

    Register your loved one with local police departments and Alzheimer's registries in your area.

If Someone Goes Missing

Immediate steps to take when a person with Alzheimer's or dementia wanders away.

1️⃣

Search the Immediate Area

Check the house, yard, and nearby areas thoroughly. Look in closets, under beds, and in other familiar hiding spots.

2️⃣

Call 911 Immediately

Don't wait. Call emergency services and provide a detailed description, recent photo, and last known location.

3️⃣

Notify Your Community

Contact neighbors, friends, and family. Post on community groups and use platforms like CarePath to spread awareness.

🚨

Emergency Contact

If you see someone with Alzheimer's or dementia who appears lost or confused, approach them calmly and call for help.
