Understanding Alzheimer's

Learn about Alzheimer's disease, its symptoms, and how we can help protect those affected by it.

What is Alzheimer's Disease?

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurological disorder that causes brain cells to degenerate and die. It is the most common cause of dementia — a continuous decline in thinking, behavioral, and social skills that affects a person's ability to function independently.

The disease is named after Dr. Alois Alzheimer, who first described it in 1906. Today, millions of people worldwide are living with Alzheimer's, and the number continues to grow as the population ages.

📊 Quick Facts

  • 60-80% of dementia cases are Alzheimer's
  • 6.7 million Americans are living with Alzheimer's
  • 1 in 3 seniors dies with Alzheimer's or another dementia
  • 60% of people with Alzheimer's will wander
  • 73% of people with Alzheimer's are age 75 or older

Common Symptoms

Recognizing the early signs of Alzheimer's can lead to earlier diagnosis and better care.

🧠

Memory Loss

Forgetting recently learned information, important dates, or events. Asking for the same information repeatedly.

🗣️

Communication Issues

Difficulty following or joining a conversation, struggling with vocabulary, or repeating themselves.

🚶

Wandering Behavior

Becoming disoriented or lost, even in familiar places. This is one of the most dangerous symptoms of Alzheimer's.

😤

Mood & Personality Changes

Becoming confused, suspicious, depressed, fearful, or anxious. Easily upset or agitated in unfamiliar situations.

👀

Visual Changes

Difficulty reading, judging distance, or determining color or contrast. May have problems with balance or coordination.

🛏️

Sleep Changes

Disrupted sleep patterns, waking up frequently at night, or experiencing sundowning (increased confusion in the evening).

Wandering Prevention Tips

Help keep your loved ones safe with these wandering prevention strategies.

🔒 Secure the Environment

  • Install locks on doors and windows
  • Use door alarms or sensors
  • Keep keys out of sight
  • Create a safe outdoor space

📱 Use Technology

  • GPS tracking devices
  • Medical alert systems
  • Video monitoring
  • ID bracelets with contact info

👥 Stay Engaged

  • Regular exercise and activities
  • Social interaction
  • Structured daily routine
  • Cognitive stimulation

📋 Plan Ahead

  • Create a care team
  • Know the local resources
  • Have a response plan
  • Notify neighbors and community
